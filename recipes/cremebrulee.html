Creme Brulee is French for 'burnt cream' which is essentially the gist of a Creme Brulee,
we make cream, then we coat it in sugar and burn the top.
I'm not going to go into technical detail with this recipe because part of the fun
of cooking is figuring it out and learning as you go.
Cooking is an intuituve art!

Ingredients

  • 6 eggs (yolks only)
  • cream
  • milk
  • sugar
  • vanilla essence

Method

  1. seperate your egg yolks from the whites into a bowl (you can save the whites to make a meringue if you desire)
  2. combine 1 part milk with 3 parts cream into a saucepan
  3. add 3tsp of vanilla essence into the cream/milk mixture
  4. put your cream mixture onto a high heat for roughly 6 minutes, you want the mixture to be near a boil
    in order to temper the eggs (once again im not going to go into detail i cbf)
  5. while your mixture heats up, whisk sugar into the egg yolks (throw in as much sugar as you'd like it's your recipe)
  6. now that you have an unhealthy amount of sugar in your yolks, your cream should be nice and hot!
  7. test the heat of your cream by dipping a finger into it, if you don't recieve 3rd degree burns, congratulations! it's time to whisk it all together
  8. gently whisk the cream mixture into the yolk mixture, do it a little bit at a time or you will cook the eggs and thats no bueno
  9. once it's all mixed in you will notice bubbles form on the surface of the mixture. kill the bubbles. we don't like bubbles.
    use a ladel to extract the bubbles and put them to the side, you can add these to your whites for a nice meringue if you'd like
  10. once the bubbles are removed, portion the combined creme mixture into ramekens
  11. place the ramekens into a bain marie

    12. HOLD UP
    WHAT THE F$*K IS A BAIN MARIE?
    my bad, i forget that we cook in the french language for some reason
    a bain marie is a special kind of bath where we use water as a sort of insulation to cook the product

  12. place your ramekens evenly spread out into a deep tray that can hold water
  13. fill the tray with hot water til the water reaches 3/4 up the sides of the ramekens (do not use boiling nor cold water)
  14. cover your tray (or bain marie hon hon hon) with tinfoil (this is one instance where it is safer to double wrap)
    make sure to tightly insulate the edges of the tray with the tinfoil
  15. place your tight tinfoil wrapped bain marie into an oven and bake it at 150C for 25mins
  16. after 25 minutes, pull your creme brulees out, you can check their readiness by giving them a shake.
    they should be gelatinous and bounce back when you lightly touch them, if they seem watery, throw them in for another 5 minutes max
  17. once your brulees are ready, it's time to cook tf out of them
  18. sprinkle a desired amount of sugar on the tops of them and blast them with heat from a gas torch

    19. don't have a gas torch? i've seen a chef use a cigarette lighter to cook the sugar before, not ideal but you gotta do what you gotta do

  19. your brulees should have a nice brown caramlised top and creamy interior

if they don't turn out nicely just keep making them again until you get it right. the best way to learn is to f$ck up and do it again.
